Research Topic

Dexterous Manipulation

Our research focuses on enabling humanoid robots to perform complex manipulation tasks with human-like dexterity. We capture fine-grained interaction data including hand poses, object states, and contact forces.

Human-Robot Interaction

The dataset includes diverse scenarios of human-robot collaboration, providing insights into natural interaction patterns and enabling the development of more intuitive robotic systems.

Abstract

We present the Humanoid Dexterous Interaction Dataset (HDID), a large-scale collection of multi-modal data capturing complex manipulation tasks performed by humanoid robots. The dataset comprises over 10,000 interaction sequences across 50+ object categories and 100+ task variations.

Each sequence includes synchronized RGB-D video, hand and body pose tracking, object 6D poses, tactile sensor readings, and force-torque measurements. This comprehensive data enables research in imitation learning, reinforcement learning, and physics-based simulation for robotic manipulation.

Our dataset addresses key challenges in dexterous manipulation including contact-rich interactions, object deformation, and multi-object coordination. We provide baseline results using state-of-the-art learning algorithms and demonstrate significant improvements in task success rates.
